"Cogito ergo sum" - Rene Descartes

Tue Nov 25, 2014

An appropriate title for Claude M. Steele's theory of exercising Self-affirmation against Stereotype Threat. Being self-aware and self-affirm one's own values that lead them to happines, fullfilment, or satisfaction is a powerful mindset that protects from performance decrement that typically arise due to stereotype threat.

When I think of the times I was the happiest and felt most fulfilled and joyful, was when I was in college. I was sharing a house with a friend and her brother (amazing people) during the week, I was practicing ballroom dancing 20 hours a week, and teaching it 10 h/week (5 of those to friends, just because:)), while also playing in MTG tourneys and making sure to practice 8-ball 2-3 hours a week (no, I am not very good at it but I did place 12th in a nationwide college 8-ball tourney, and yes there were only 14 female players:D ). Looking back at those 2 years, I can easily say those years were pretty good years. And when I think about the values, a bunch of them stand out:

  • Close relationships/family: This was important. Sharing the wealth, knowledge, and joy just adds to itself. The more (Honest and Open) people to share these with, the more they get.
  • Ambition: I loved ballroom dancing, still do. And though it was physically tiring, I never felt "tired". Ambition is like a warm (not burning) fire that warmed me up and just fueled me back up whenever I felt a bit tired. Like Confucius said "Choose a job you love, and you will never have to work a day."
  • Knowledge & Growth: I just love to learn. Especially learn more about things I love, and to grow and expand in the process, mentally, emotionally, and psychologically.
  • Mentorship & Helping others: This comes back to sharing, but for me they are more than that. There is a joy I find in sharing what I know so far, and in helping others.
  • And Freedom to do all of these, what I deem right to do.

These values indirectly led to other values which are just as important: The presence of these values increased my Confidence, Inner Harmony, and Serenity. I was Effective (I love efficiency to an extend where I can't stand inefficiency) and thus was Accomplishing much.

If I had to measure myself how much I try to live up to these values in general now with the following scale:

Disagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree

  • Close relationships/family : 5
  • Ambition : 4
  • Knowledge & Growth : 5
  • Mentorship & Helping others : 4
  • Freedom : 3

Writing about the happiest times in my life made me remember, made me re-feel how I felt back then. Looking at the table(s) above, gave me a better perspective about the reasons.

I know that remembering these values, being aware that I have them and can use them will help mediate stereotype threat if it happens to present itself, and help me in case I feel down at DBC.

I know, therefore I am.
